Abstract

EconGym is a scalable and modular simulation testbed that bridges artificial intelligence (AI) and economics, enabling rigorous training, evaluation, and benchmarking of AI algorithms in realistic economic settings.

Unlike existing environments that focus on narrow or stylized tasks, EconGym is built upon rigorous economic theory and supports over 25 composable economic scenarios involving heterogeneous agents—such as households, firms, banks, and governments. It empowers users to simulate dynamic, multi-agent economic trajectories by flexibly assigning roles and learning algorithms, including reinforcement learning (RL), large language models (LLMs), behavior cloning (BC), rule-based strategies, real-world policies, and more.

Whether you are an economist looking to conduct economic analysis or experiment with AI algorithms, or an AI researcher aiming to develop new algorithms in complex scenarios such as hierarchical, heterogeneous, and large-scale agent dynamics, EconGym offers a powerful playground for tackling real-world economic challenges—from pension reform and taxation to monetary coordination and beyond.

Key Contributions

  • Theory-grounded & extensible modeling: Models core economic roles—households, firms, banks, and governments—using advanced micro/macro theory, including heterogeneity (e.g., OLG households, Ramsey consumers, monopolistic firms, central banks).
  • Cross-domain task composition: Tasks are constructed from modular economic roles with well-defined observations, actions, and rewards, enabling customizable and realistic economic simulations across domains such as tax, pension, labor, and monetary policy.
  • Multi-algorithm support & benchmarking: Seamlessly integrates diverse agent types—RL, LLMs, BC, economic solvers, and real-data agents—supporting reproducible comparisons and hybrid strategies.
  • Scalability & realism: Efficiently simulates large-scale agent populations (up to 10,000 agents) with high fidelity, supporting realistic dynamics for policy learning and analysis.
  • A unified testbed for economics × AI: Empowers both AI researchers and economists to jointly explore complex policy spaces, benchmark algorithmic strategies, and study emergent economic behavior in multi-agent settings.

User Manual

We provide a detailed User Manual that covers 25 key economic research questions, each accompanied by:

  • An introduction to the economic issue
  • Recommended choices of economic roles and agent algorithms
  • Baseline experimental results and visualizations
  • A YAML configuration file for running the environment directly

🔗 See the document/ folder for full documentation and research use cases.
